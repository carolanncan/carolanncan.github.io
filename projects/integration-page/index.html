Segment Integrations

Clarify the workflow for sending data to new analytics tools in the Segment dashboard.

The problem:

Failing to communicate helfpul information.

Some integrations can receive data from Segment with our out-of-the-box implementation. But many tools need to be instrumented in a way that requires extra steps and instruction.

We were failing to communicate required extra steps in the interface, which created a point of failure for our users. The lack of clarity around what extra steps are necessary and which integrations required the extra steps was creating a confusing and frustrating experience for customers.

Users can't troubelshoot on their own.

Users could not troubleshoot independently which created frustration for them and a heavy work load for our support team.

Looking into the future, we'll be adding even more integrations to the platform that will require a few extra steps of set up. With this in mind, the process needs to be really clear.

The existing workflow is confusing.

There were a few qualities we wanted to retain in the interaction:

  • The feeling of something you just want to click on
  • Sending data to an integration feels effortless.
  • It’s an interface element that a lot of people are familiar with.
**Things that are not working well:** - “Turning on” and “turning off” a tool is not the best mindset to put our customers in when they’re making the decision to start or stop sending data to an integration. - We want Segment to become more of a marketplace for customers and partners. With the toggle, the product feels a bit more like a switchboard. - The **tile **and the **toggle** are each clickable but have two, slightly different outcomes, which creates some confusion. **Why did we remove the toggle right now?** - The integrations-platform team has been working on a project (+ Platform Mobile and + Platform Integration Sheets Enhancements ) to make a few small improvements to the experience of our mobile integrations. - The integrations page has a lot of known issues. In trying to work around those issues for the current project, it became harder to justify keeping the toggle than to remove it. **What about all of our messaging about the toggle and the “flip of a switch”?** - This creates work for our marketing team and anyone involved in external communication to update messaging about Segment. - We think we can make a graceful transition away from the toggle by making incremental updates. A toggle is more commonly used for low-stakes decisions/actions. Here’s an example from Spotify. - These updates haven’t been tested on users yet. They are a quick band-aid for some known or obvious issues. More thorough updates are coming next!
