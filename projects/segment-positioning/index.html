Positioning Segment

The challenge for this project was to figure out how to answer, "What is Segment?" without wanting to...

Problems to solve for:

To be able to answer that question we needed to identify exactly why we were not already great at doing that.

To dig into that problem and how it might be fixed, we interviewed a few people on our team, pulled from what we learned in Jobs to Be Done research, and sifted through Zendesk tickets.

We boiled it down to three specific problems we can take action on right now:

  • We’ve been inconsistent in how we communicate the value of Segment.

  • We’ve strayed from our target audience and, thus, set incorrect expectations of our product.

  • Because of this, messaging has become a burden for all of our teams.

Defining the target audience

The jobs-to-be-done research reaffirmed that Segment's best audience (for now) is a combination of someone with a somewhat technical skillset who is also business-minded. Segment's messaging will be most effective when it resonates with that audience.

Refining the value propositions

We interviewed a few members of the team to see how we talk about Segment internally. Turns out… none of us really describe Segment in the same way! It was clear that we’re not fully aligned on what the core value propositions of the product are. To fix that, we referred to the “Push” and “Draw” forces that came from the customer interviews we did in the jobs-to-be-done research.

Those forces acted as a laundry list of the primary ways our customers are receiving value from Segment. We grouped those values by our customer cohorts (from customer jobs), common themes of the values, and made sure they lined up with our product vision.

We then consolidated the larger list into a grouping of four value propositions that are core to the focus of our product:

  • Use one API for tracking data

  • Send your data to many tools and databases

  • Collect customer data from multiple sources

  • Access and own your raw data

Improving our messaging

We found a parallel between the general order in which customers experience the values of our product and the order in which they experience pain points using data.

As customers transition from getting their feet wet with analytics all the way to asking more complex questions of their data and need access to their raw data, we can see how the resulting timeline helps us know how and when to communicate the right information to our customers. It also helps us anticipate what their next steps might be and how we can help them be successful.

How Segment put this to use

Product

The product team began to focus on increasing the quality of the very first value proposition we defined. This ensured that every new customer we brought in would have a great first impression of our brand, and existing customers would become more confident in the product they already love.

Sales

This research has helped the sales team speak from a more informed place when they're starting conversations with new leads. The timeline has been valuable for pinpointing where a prospect is on the timeline and having an educated idea of what the prospect's current problems, constraints, and technical knowledge are.

For example, if they are early in the timeline, we can hint at the problems they might run into down the line, and bring focus to how we help them solve the those problems.

Growth and Success

We can ensure our customers have an excellent experience solving their problems at each stage. We can tailor our docs and help content to the customers at each stage of the timeline, and where appropriate, we can offer advice in support tickets that help customers advance to the next level.

Marketing

Segment's content strategy is to become the default resource for data analysis information, implementation, and best practices. This research helped us understand how to design the marketing site to speak to people both the in the early and late stages of the timeline.

This research also validated some early learnings the team had about producing content that speaks to best practices for our industry, in general, rather than only producing content pertaining to best practices for the use of our product.
